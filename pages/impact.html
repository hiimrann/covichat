Covid-19's impact on our society

Covid-19 has revealed persistent inequalities in income, age, race, gender, and geographic location. Despite recent advances in global health, people around the world continue to face complex and interconnected health and well-being threats that stem from the social, economic, political, and environmental determinants of health.

The pandemic also revealed significant gaps in the national health information system. While resource-rich environments face challenges related to capacity overload and fragmentation, weaker health systems have the potential to jeopardize the hard-won health and development gains of recent decades.

Today, the impact of Covid-19 on daily life is extensive and has far-reaching effects.

    On medical care:
  • Challenges in the diagnosis, isolation and treatment of suspected or confirmed cases
  • High burden on the functioning of the existing medical system
  • Patients with other diseases and health problems neglected
  • The burden on doctors and other health professionals is very high and they are at very high risk
  • Medical stores are overloaded
  • They need high protection
  • Disruption of the medical supply chain
  • People suffering from mental illness
    On the economy:
  • Slowing the production of manufacturers
  • Product supply chain disruption
  • National and international trade losses
  • Poor cash flow in the market
  • Significant slowdown in revenue growth
    On the society:
  • Sector unable to provide
  • Cancellation or postponement of major sporting events.
  • Avoid cancellations and services of national and international trips
  • Interruption of cultural, religious and festive celebrations
  • Excessive pressure on the public
  • Maintain social distance with colleagues and family
  • Close hotels, restaurants and places of worship
  • The venue is closed for entertainment venues, such as cinemas, theaters, sports clubs, stadiums, swimming pools, etc
    On education:
  • Significant disruptions have occurred in education sector of the world since beginning of January 2020 due to Covid-19 pandemic
  • At the height of the pandemic, 45 countries in Europe and Central Asia closed schools, affecting 185 million students
  • One of the constraints of emergency distance learning is the lack of personal interaction between teachers and students
  • Postponement of exams
