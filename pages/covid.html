What is Covid-19?

Covid-19 respiratory disease which is caused by a new strain of coronavirus and affects the with mild to severe health conditions. It is a disease which doesn’t require special treatment if the conditions of a Covid patients are mild.

However, older people, and those with underlying medical problems like cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, and cancer are more likely to suffer serious symptoms and die as a result.

What is a pandemic?

A pandemic refers to a disease that occurs throughout many continents or worldwide. Majority of the people in the world suffering from the same disease or is at risk of receiving the same disease is known as pandemic.

History of Covid

Covid-19 started as an epidemic in 31st of December 2019 in the region of Wuhan, China. Although WHO (World Health Organization) didn’t immediately announce it as an epidemic however, on 11th of March 2020 they were forced to announce it as an epidemic due to the fact that majority of the countries in the world started receiving the news of Covid cases in their nations.

China had been the first country with a widespread outbreak in January, and South Korea, Iran and Italy following in February with outbreaks of coronavirus in. Soon, the virus put its claws in all continents and over 177 countries. Covid-19 impacted the lives of all the population around the world in bad and difficult ways. All though the consequences of these impacts affected in different manners depending on the individuals’ status in the society.
